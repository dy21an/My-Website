Healthy Recipes

Nutritious and delicious meals to fuel your fitness journey

Nutrition for Success

Proper nutrition is just as important as exercise when it comes to achieving your fitness goals. These recipes are designed to provide the nutrients your body needs to perform, recover, and thrive. All recipes are high in protein, balanced in macronutrients, and packed with vitamins and minerals.

Breakfast Recipes

High-Protein Pancakes

Prep: 10 mins | Cook: 15 mins | Serves: 2

Calories: 350 Protein: 28g Carbs: 42g Fat: 8g

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup oats
  • 2 scoops vanilla protein powder
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 banana, mashed
  • 1/2 cup almond milk
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Blend oats into flour consistency
  2. Mix all ingredients in a bowl until smooth
  3. Heat non-stick pan over medium heat
  4. Pour batter to form pancakes
  5. Cook 2-3 minutes each side until golden
  6. Serve with fresh berries and Greek yogurt

Power Veggie Omelette

Prep: 5 mins | Cook: 10 mins | Serves: 1

Calories: 280 Protein: 24g Carbs: 12g Fat: 16g

Ingredients:

  • 3 whole eggs
  • 1/2 cup spinach, chopped
  • 1/4 cup bell peppers, diced
  • 1/4 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 tbsp feta cheese
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Beat eggs in a bowl with salt and pepper
  2. Heat olive oil in pan over medium heat
  3. Sauté vegetables until tender
  4. Pour eggs over vegetables
  5. Cook until edges set, add cheese
  6. Fold and serve immediately

Lunch Recipes

Grilled Chicken Power Salad

Prep: 15 mins | Cook: 20 mins | Serves: 2

Calories: 420 Protein: 38g Carbs: 28g Fat: 18g

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts (200g each)
  • 4 cups mixed greens
  • 1 cup quinoa, cooked
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup almonds, sliced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Lemon juice and herbs

Instructions:

  1. Season chicken with herbs, salt, and pepper
  2. Grill chicken 6-7 minutes per side
  3. Let chicken rest, then slice
  4. Arrange greens, quinoa, and vegetables
  5. Top with sliced chicken and avocado
  6. Drizzle with olive oil and lemon juice

Teriyaki Salmon Bowl

Prep: 10 mins | Cook: 20 mins | Serves: 2

Calories: 480 Protein: 32g Carbs: 48g Fat: 16g

Ingredients:

  • 2 salmon fillets (150g each)
  • 1 cup brown rice, cooked
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 1 cup edamame
  • 2 tbsp teriyaki sauce (low sodium)
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds
  • Green onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Brush salmon with teriyaki sauce
  2. Bake salmon at 400°F for 12-15 minutes
  3. Steam broccoli and edamame
  4. Divide rice into bowls
  5. Top with salmon and vegetables
  6. Garnish with sesame seeds and green onions

Dinner Recipes

Lean Beef Stir Fry

Prep: 15 mins | Cook: 15 mins | Serves: 3

Calories: 380 Protein: 34g Carbs: 32g Fat: 12g

Ingredients:

  • 400g lean beef sirloin, sliced
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (bell peppers, snap peas, carrots)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 cup brown rice, cooked
  • Fresh ginger, grated

Instructions:

  1. Heat sesame oil in wok over high heat
  2. Stir-fry beef 3-4 minutes, remove and set aside
  3. Add vegetables, garlic, and ginger
  4. Stir-fry vegetables 4-5 minutes
  5. Return beef to wok, add soy sauce
  6. Serve over brown rice

Healthy Turkey Meatballs

Prep: 20 mins | Cook: 25 mins | Serves: 4

Calories: 320 Protein: 36g Carbs: 24g Fat: 10g

Ingredients:

  • 500g lean ground turkey
  • 1/2 cup oats
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 2 cups marinara sauce (low sugar)
  • Whole wheat pasta

Instructions:

  1. Mix turkey, oats, egg, garlic, and seasoning
  2. Form into 16 meatballs
  3. Bake at 400°F for 20-25 minutes
  4. Heat marinara sauce in pan
  5. Add cooked meatballs to sauce
  6. Serve over whole wheat pasta

Snacks & Smoothies

Post-Workout Protein Smoothie

Prep: 5 mins | Serves: 1

Calories: 280 Protein: 30g Carbs: 32g Fat: 4g

Ingredients:

  • 1 scoop vanilla protein powder
  • 1 banana
  • 1 cup spinach
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 tbsp almond butter
  • 1/2 cup frozen berries
  • Ice cubes

Instructions:

  1. Add all ingredients to blender
  2. Blend on high for 60 seconds
  3. Add more milk if too thick
  4. Pour and enjoy immediately

No-Bake Energy Balls

Prep: 15 mins | Makes: 12 balls

Calories: 95 Protein: 4g Carbs: 12g Fat: 4g

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup oats
  • 1/2 cup natural peanut butter
  • 1/3 cup honey
  • 1/3 cup dark chocolate chips
  • 1/4 cup ground flaxseed
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Mix all ingredients in bowl
  2. Refrigerate mixture 30 minutes
  3. Roll into 12 equal balls
  4. Store in refrigerator up to 1 week
  5. Perfect pre or post-workout snack

Nutrition Tips for Fitness Success

Stay Hydrated

Drink at least 8-10 glasses of water daily. Increase intake during workouts and hot weather.

Time Your Meals

Eat a balanced meal 2-3 hours before exercise and refuel within 30 minutes post-workout.

Prioritize Protein

Aim for 1.6-2.2g of protein per kg of body weight daily to support muscle growth and recovery.

Choose Whole Foods

Focus on unprocessed foods like vegetables, fruits, lean proteins, and whole grains.
